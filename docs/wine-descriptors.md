## Texture & Structure
- **Lush** – Sumptuously rich and soft on the palate, often low in acidity but full of ripe fruit and a cushiony texture.
- **Voluptuous** – Luxuriously full-bodied and caressing in mouthfeel; indulgent in flavor and texture.
- **Seductive** – Alluring wine with a soft, inviting texture and an engaging aromatic profile.
- **Linear** – A focused, direct wine that evolves in a clean, straight progression without broadness.
- **Layered** – A wine showing multiple distinct aromatic and flavor elements that unfold over time.
- **Textbook** – A perfect and clear example of its varietal or regional type.
- **Opulent** – Rich and lavish, displaying an extravagant depth of fruit, often with a silky, plush mouthfeel.

## Acidity & Freshness
- **Vibrant** – Full of energy, with lively acidity that gives freshness and vitality.
- **Racy** – Marked by high, mouthwatering acidity, typically giving a sense of energy and lift.
- **Zesty** – Bright and citrus-like acidity, refreshing and clean.
- **Piercing** – Intensely sharp acidity that cuts distinctly through the palate.

## Fruit Descriptors

### Tree Fruits
- **Apple** – Fresh orchard fruit ranging from tart green to sweet red varieties, common in crisp whites.
- **Green Apple** – Tart, crisp fruit indicating high acidity and freshness in cool-climate whites.
- **Pear** – Soft, subtle orchard fruit often found in Pinot Grigio and Albariño.
- **Peach** – Stone fruit flavor indicating ripeness, common in Viognier and aged Chardonnay.
- **Apricot** – Delicate stone fruit, often in aromatic whites and dessert wines.
- **Quince** – Aromatic, slightly tart fruit with floral notes, found in aged whites.

### Citrus Fruits
- **Lemon** – Bright citrus providing freshness and acidity in crisp whites.
- **Lime** – Zesty, sharp citrus common in Sauvignon Blanc and Verdejo.
- **Grapefruit** – Tangy citrus with slight bitterness, characteristic of Sauvignon Blanc.
- **Orange** – Sweet citrus notes, often orange zest in aromatic whites.
- **Meyer Lemon** – Sweeter, less acidic lemon character in refined whites.

### Red & Black Fruits
- **Cherry** – Fresh red fruit common in Pinot Noir and Sangiovese.
- **Black Cherry** – Rich, dark fruit with slight tartness in mature reds.
- **Blackberry** – Dark, jammy fruit typical of ripe Cabernet and Syrah.
- **Raspberry** – Bright red berry with sweet-tart character in Grenache.
- **Strawberry** – Sweet red berry often found in rosé and light reds.
- **Cassis** – Black currant, hallmark of quality Cabernet Sauvignon.
- **Plum** – Soft, sweet dark fruit in medium-bodied reds like Merlot.
- **Blueberry** – Sweet dark berry common in warm-climate Syrah.
- **Cranberry** – Tart red fruit indicating bright acidity in light reds.

### Tropical Fruits
- **Pineapple** – Tropical sweetness in ripe Chardonnay and Chenin Blanc.
- **Mango** – Exotic, sweet tropical fruit in aromatic varieties.
- **Passion Fruit** – Intense tropical aroma in New World Sauvignon Blanc.
- **Lychee** – Distinctive sweet-floral fruit in Gewürztraminer.
- **Guava** – Tropical fruit with musky sweetness in aromatic whites.

### Dried & Cooked Fruits
- **Fig** – Sweet, jammy character in aged reds and fortified wines.
- **Raisin** – Dried grape sweetness in late-harvest and aged wines.
- **Prune** – Dried plum notes indicating very ripe or aged character.
- **Brambly** – Fruit aromas reminiscent of wild blackberries or raspberries, often slightly earthy.
- **Stewed** – Fruit notes that taste cooked or preserved, often suggesting warmth or overripeness.
- **Fleshy** – Ripe and succulent fruit character, giving a juicy and rich impression.
- **Macerated** – Fruit flavors that are softened and intensified by soaking, often appearing richer or sweeter.

## Tannins & Finish
- **Supple** – Tannins that feel smooth and flexible, gently structuring the palate without astringency.
- **Grippy** – Noticeably firm tannins that cling to the palate, often giving a drying sensation.
- **Velvety** – Extremely smooth tannins with a plush, soft mouthfeel.
- **Dusty** – Fine-grained, dry tannins reminiscent of powdered texture, subtle but lingering.
- **Chewy** – Rich, dense tannins that create a sensation of substantial texture.

## Complexity & Development
- **Multifaceted** – Offering numerous distinct aromas and flavors, often indicative of high complexity.
- **Brooding** – Deep and somewhat closed initially, revealing more complexity and layers with time.
- **Nuanced** – Subtle complexities that require careful attention to fully appreciate.
- **Evolving** – Continuously changing and unfolding new characteristics over time.

## Earth & Mineral
- **Flinty** – Stony or mineral aroma, reminiscent of struck flint or wet stones.
- **Graphite** – Mineral aroma similar to pencil lead, often associated with structured, mineral-driven wines.
- **Loamy** – Earthy aroma reminiscent of fertile, rich soil.
- **Chalky** – Mineral note suggesting crushed chalk, often accompanied by a dry texture.

## Nuts & Seeds
- **Hazelnut** – Toasted, sweet nut character often from oak aging or oxidative development.
- **Almond** – Delicate nut flavor, can be sweet or bitter, common in aged whites.
- **Walnut** – Rich, slightly bitter nut character in aged wines.
- **Chestnut** – Sweet, earthy nut flavor in mature wines.
- **Brazil Nut** – Rich, creamy nut character in aged Chardonnay.
- **Marcona Almond** – Buttery, sweet Spanish almond character in aged whites.

## Oak & Spice
- **Toasty** – Aromas suggesting toasted oak, similar to freshly baked bread or lightly browned nuts.
- **Vanilla** – Sweet spice from oak aging, especially American oak.
- **Cinnamon** – Warm baking spice from oak or grape variety.
- **Clove** – Warm, sweet, aromatic spice note, commonly found in wines aged in oak.
- **Nutmeg** – Sweet, warm spice often from oak influence.
- **Black Pepper** – Spicy, pungent note characteristic of Syrah and Grüner Veltliner.
- **White Pepper** – Milder pepper spice in aromatic whites.
- **Allspice** – Spice aromas combining elements of cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.
- **Vanillin** – Sweet spice aroma derived from oak aging, distinctly reminiscent of vanilla.
- **Cedar** – Wood aroma, delicate and aromatic, similar to pencil shavings or cedar boxes.
- **Spicy** – Aromas or flavors reminiscent of spices such as pepper, cinnamon, cloves, or nutmeg, often adding complexity and depth.

## Aromatics & Floral
- **Rose** – Classic floral note in aromatic varieties like Gewürztraminer.
- **Violet** – Delicate floral typical of Nebbiolo and Syrah.
- **Lavender** – Aromatic purple flower scent in Rhône blends.
- **Honeysuckle** – Sweet floral aroma in aromatic whites.
- **Orange Blossom** – Citrus flower aroma in Mediterranean whites.
- **Jasmine** – Intense white flower aroma in aromatic varieties.
- **Elderflower** – Delicate, muscat-like floral in aromatic whites.
- **Lifted** – Bright, pronounced aromatic intensity, often floral or fruity in nature.
- **Perfumed** – Intense and attractive aromas, often floral or exotic.
- **Exotic** – Aromas and flavors that are intriguing and unusual, often tropical or floral in nature.

## Herbs & Vegetables
- **Thyme** – Savory herb common in Mediterranean wines.
- **Rosemary** – Aromatic herb in Rhône and Provence wines.
- **Sage** – Earthy herb character in rustic reds.
- **Mint** – Fresh, cooling herb often in Australian Cabernet.
- **Eucalyptus** – Minty, medicinal aroma in Australian wines.
- **Green Bell Pepper** – Vegetal note in underripe Cabernet.
- **Asparagus** – Green vegetable note in Sauvignon Blanc.
- **Grass** – Fresh-cut grass in crisp whites.
- **Hay** – Dried grass aroma in aged whites.

## Sweetness & Balance
- **Off-dry** – Slightly sweet taste, not completely dry but only subtly sweet.
- **Rounded** – Harmonious balance, with no harsh edges, integrated sweetness, acidity, and tannins.
- **Polished** – Smooth, well-integrated components creating a refined, balanced impression.

## Alcohol & Body
- **Warming** – Noticeable alcohol warmth on the palate, often creating a sense of richness or heat.
- **Heady** – Potent, intense alcoholic impression, giving a robust and powerful sensation.
