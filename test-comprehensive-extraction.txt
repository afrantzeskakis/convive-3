Classic French Coq au Vin

Ingredients:
- 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
- 6 strips thick-cut bacon
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 1 bottle red wine (Burgundy preferred)
- 8 oz pearl onions, peeled
- 8 oz button mushrooms, quartered
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 bay leaves
- Fresh thyme sprigs
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 2 tablespoons butter
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Fresh parsley, chopped for garnish

Instructions:
1. Cook bacon in a heavy Dutch oven until crispy. Remove and set aside.
2. Season chicken pieces with salt and pepper. Brown in bacon fat on all sides.
3. Add tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
4. Deglaze with red wine, scraping up browned bits from bottom of pan.
5. Add bay leaves and thyme sprigs. Bring to a simmer.
6. Cover and braise in 325Â°F oven for 45 minutes.
7. Add pearl onions and mushrooms. Continue cooking 15 minutes.
8. Remove chicken and vegetables. Strain sauce and reduce if needed.
9. Whisk butter into sauce to create glossy finish.
10. Return chicken to sauce and garnish with fresh parsley.

Traditional French technique requiring patience and proper mise en place.
